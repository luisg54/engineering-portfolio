Overview

A collection of CAD design work demonstrating proficiency in SolidWorks, full drawing packages per ASME Y14.5 GD&T standards, and assembly design with BOM documentation. The featured project is a complete mechanical design of a foosball table — from concept through fabrication-ready drawings.

  • Table frame detailed with all critical dimensions, tapped holes, and tolerances — fractional ±1/20–1/32", angular ±0.0001°, two/three-place decimal per ASME

  • All parts modeled in SolidWorks; drawings produced to ASME Y14.5 GD&T standard
Lessons Learned
A drawing is a manufacturing contract
Every dimension and tolerance on a drawing is a commitment to the machinist. Under-tolerancing creates parts that don't fit; over-tolerancing drives up cost unnecessarily. Learning to apply GD&T intentionally — not just to pass a course — meant understanding what each callout actually controls on the manufactured part.
Source hardware before you model it
I modeled a custom needle-roller thrust bearing before checking McMaster-Carr. The standard part was cheaper, available next day, and already had a CAD model. Default to standard hardware and only go custom when you have to.
